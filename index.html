Our Story

Milltown Tex Mex & Grill
A Neighborhood Spot with Real Roots

Milltown Tex Mex & Grill isn't trying to be fancy — it's trying to be real. Built around the kind of food that speaks for itself, pulling from traditional Mexican cooking, family-style recipes, and the meals you grow up with.

This isn't a trimmed-down menu designed for trends. It's big, a little chaotic, and full of options because it reflects how people actually eat.

Bold flavors, real community, cold drinks. Come hungry. Leave happy.

Milltown Tex Mex & Grill

A Neighborhood Spot with Real Roots

Established in 2023, Milltown Tex Mex & Grill isn't trying to be fancy — it's trying to be real.

Built around the kind of food that speaks for itself, the menu pulls from traditional Mexican cooking, family-style recipes, and the kind of meals you grow up with. You see it in the variety — tacos filled with everything from classic al pastor to slow-braised birria, cemitas stacked the way they're meant to be, and plates from Oaxaca like mole and enchiladas, that take time to get just right.

This isn't a trimmed-down menu designed for trends. It's big, a little chaotic, and full of options because it reflects how people actually eat — mixing comfort food, street food, and full meals all in one place.

You'll also notice something else: it's part grill, part taqueria, part breakfast spot. That's intentional. It's built for regulars — people who stop in for a quick sandwich one day and come back for tacos or a full plate the next.

At its core, Milltown Tex Mex & Grill feels like it was built by someone who didn't want to overcomplicate things — just serve generous portions of food that hits the mark, every time.
